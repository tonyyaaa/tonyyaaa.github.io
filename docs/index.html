Windsor, Ontario - Near Via Italia

Born and raised Italian-Canadian, Tony Alameda is a proud son of Windsor's Via Italia neighbourhood. A 28-year member of the Caboto Club, a bocce ball regular at Jackson Park, and a man who takes his Sunday gravy seriously - tomato sauce only, no exceptions.

A Villanova Catholic alumnus (his mother's orders - no son of hers would attend that public Protestant garbage), Tony keeps Windsor's traditions alive. You'll find him most Wednesday evenings at the Legion Hall in his signature red golf shirt, polka dancing with the dignity of a man who knows exactly who he is.

Tony is also a proud Volvo enthusiast and a custodian - a man who maintains things, keeps them running, believes in order and cleanliness and the importance of showing up.
